WEC-Pro — Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1) What is WEC-Pro?

WEC-Pro is a research project that advances wave energy converters (WECs) using large-scale additive manufacturing (LSAM). It integrates numerical modeling, experimental testing, techno-economic assessment, and LSAM to deliver reliable, cost-effective marine energy solutions.

2) Why is wave energy important?

Ocean waves offer an underused global resource (~32 PWh/year). They complement wind and solar in timing and predictability, helping reduce land use, strengthen energy security, and support climate goals.

3) What are today’s main challenges?

  • High costs for design, manufacturing, and multi-scale testing.
  • Methods borrowed from naval/offshore engineering not tailored to WEC needs.
  • Reliability and bankability hurdles at low Technology Readiness Levels (TRLs).

4) How does LSAM help?

  • Rapid, lower-cost prototyping of complex WEC geometries.
  • Reduced material waste and option to use recycled materials.
  • Scalable production without molds, enabling faster design iterations.

5) What are WEC-Pro’s goals?

  • Create an integrated methodology combining modeling, testing, economics, and LSAM.
  • Optimize and validate two benchmark WECs (PivWEC and PeWEC) via scaled tank tests.
  • Raise TRL while cutting uncertainty, cycle time, and cost.
  • Provide tools transferrable to other marine renewables (tidal, hybrid wind-wave, wave-solar).

6) Who is involved?

The project is hosted at Politecnico di Torino (POLITO), with Caracol (LSAM specialist SME) and INSEAN (Italy’s national hydrodynamics/wave-tank facility) as key partners.

7) What impact is expected?

  • Scientific: Open methods, datasets, and software; journal articles; conference papers.
  • Societal: Outreach via fairs, media, and open events to build ocean-energy awareness.
  • Economic: Pipeline for collaborative R&D, potential start-up activity, and contribution to EU offshore targets.

8) How does WEC-Pro align with EU priorities?

WEC-Pro supports the European Green Deal and REPowerEU, and advances SDGs 7 (clean energy), 9 (industry & innovation), 11 (sustainable communities), and 13 (climate action).
